Inside the Frame

I've always had a good eye — first through a camera, now through code. Design has been part of my life since I was a kid, playing with layouts and writing code just to see what would happen. These days, it's more focused: clean, functional websites made for small businesses and artists who are out there doing the real work.

Flint & Frame is my next chapter — a way to work with purpose, support the community I care about, and claim a little more freedom in the process. I take each project seriously because I know what it means to put something of your own out into the world.

Tools Change. Principles Don't.

I start every project with pen and paper — it helps me slow down, think clearly, and design with purpose. I've always leaned towards a back to basics mindset. The tools are digital, but the approach is analog: listen first, sketch it out, then build it right.

I believe good design doesn't need to shout. It just needs to work, to feel right, and to support real people doing real work. Every project gets the same care I'd give my own — thoughtful, focused and built to last.

Our life is frittered away by detail... Simplify, simplify.

— Henry David Thoreau
